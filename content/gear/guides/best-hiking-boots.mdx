Choosing the right hiking boots is perhaps the most important gear decision. Wrong boots ruin hikes. Right boots last years and prevent blisters, joint damage, and misery.

## Low-Cut vs Mid-Cut Boots

Low-cut boots like the Salomon X Ultra 4 are lighter and more comfortable for easy terrain. They're perfect for Veluwe or Heuvelrug day hikes on good trails. They don't restrict ankle movement and are faster and more responsive.

Mid-cut boots like the Scarpa Zodiac Plus provide ankle support crucial for steep, technical terrain. They're mandatory for routes like Drielandenpunt-Geuldal or the Ourthe Valley where uneven, rocky ground dominates. The ankle support prevents rolling injuries and provides confidence on challenging descents.

## Gore-Tex vs Non-Waterproof

Gore-Tex waterproofing is transformative in the Benelux where rain is constant and unpredictable. Waterproof boots keep feet dry, preventing blisters and hypothermia. Gore-Tex breathes reasonably well—moisture management is good despite being waterproof.

Non-waterproof boots dry faster once wet but get wet constantly in Benelux conditions. Only consider non-waterproof if you're hiking dry terrain (rare here) or prefer the lowest weight possible.

## Fit & Break-In

Always try boots before purchase. Different brands fit differently. Scarpa tends narrow, Salomon true-to-size, Keen wide.

Budget 50-100km break-in for any new boot. Blisters during break-in are normal. Use Leukoplast blister prevention tape on hot spots. After break-in, good boots become invisible—you simply forget you're wearing them.

## Budget Considerations

Premium boots (€200+) use better materials, last longer, and fit better. Budget boots (€100-150) work fine but need careful fitting and may not last as long.

For serious hikers: invest in quality. Replace every 1,500-2,000km (typically every 2-3 years of regular use). For casual hikers: budget boots work fine if properly fitted.

## Our Recommendations

- **Easy day hikes**: Salomon X Ultra 4 (lightweight, responsive, comfortable)
- **Hilly terrain**: Scarpa Zodiac Plus (excellent ankle support, durability, precision fit)
- **Budget option**: Keen Targhee III (wide toe box, good value, solid waterproofing)
