A good daypack is invisible. It shouldn't be noticed after hours of hiking. A bad daypack becomes torture after 2 hours.

## Pack Size

**15L packs** are for 2-3 hour hikes with minimal gear. Only viable if you're local to the trailhead and absolutely minimalist. Too small for most situations.

**22L packs** are perfect for 5-8 hour full-day hikes. Room for layers, snacks, hydration, first aid, and emergency gear. The sweet spot for most day hikers. The Osprey Talon 22 is the standard here.

**30L packs** are for long days (8+ hours) or lighter backpacking. Room for more food, water, and equipment. Slightly heavier and bulkier but still manageable for day hikes.

## Key Features

**Back Panel**: Ventilated or mesh backs are significantly more comfortable than solid panels. Air circulation prevents sweat accumulation and reduces heat build-up.

**Hipbelt**: A good hipbelt transfers weight from shoulders to hips where you're stronger. Crucial for comfort on long hikes.

**Side Pockets**: Water bottle pockets are mandatory. Quick access to hydration is essential.

**Top Lid**: Covers the opening and provides security. Many packs have removable lids for further flexibility.

**Organization**: Multiple small pockets keep things organized. Too many pockets create chaos; too few creates rummaging.

## Materials & Durability

Good daypacks are typically made from Cordura or similar durable nylon. They should last 5+ years of regular use.

Weight matters less than comfort. A 100g heavier pack with better ergonomics will feel lighter than a lighter pack with poor design.
