Germany's hiking infrastructure is world-class. The Black Forest is an icon of German romanticism—dense fir forests, traditional farmhouses with steep roofs, and the legendary Westweg long-distance trail.

Saxon Switzerland offers dramatic sandstone formations, narrow gorges, and breathtaking viewpoints. This region is beloved by German hikers and remains less crowded than the Alps.

From traditional hiking villages to pristine nature reserves, German hiking combines excellent organization with authentic alpine experience.
