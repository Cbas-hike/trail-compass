Belgium punches far above its weight in hiking quality. The Belgian Ardennes rivals the Swiss Alps in drama, while the High Fens offer a unique bogland ecosystem found nowhere else in Western Europe.

The Semois Valley is a hidden gem—dramatic river meanders carved through beech and oak forests, with tobacco-farming villages frozen in time. Belgium's hiking culture is deeply rooted, with centuries-old GR trails and local walking associations maintaining the extensive path network.

You'll find yourself hiking past medieval castles, through UNESCO-protected landscapes, and across some of Europe's most authentic, unspoiled countryside.
