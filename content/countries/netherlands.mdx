The Netherlands is Europe's most underrated hiking destination. While many know it for its cycling, the Dutch landscape offers incredible diversity: vast heathlands in the Veluwe, rolling hills in Limburg, dramatic dunes along the coast, and serene forest paths connecting historic villages.

The Dutch have perfected the art of accessible, well-marked hiking. Every route is clearly signposted with distance markers, usually featuring convenient loops and excellent public transport connections.

From the Veluwe's ancient forests to the three-country point at Drielandenpunt, expect pristine nature within easy reach of major cities.
