The Netherlands offers exceptional hiking despite its small size. From vast heathlands to dramatic river valleys, Dutch hiking provides remarkable diversity within short distances.

## Top Destinations

**Veluwe**: Europe's largest inland sand dunes and the famous Hoge Veluwe National Park. Perfect for all skill levels. The heathland bloom in August-September is spectacular.

**Zuid-Limburg**: The Netherlands' only mountainous region with real elevation, dramatic valleys, and the three-country point at Drielandenpunt. Best for serious hikers.

**Utrechtse Heuvelrug**: Forested ridge between Amsterdam and Arnhem with castles and country estates. Excellent for cultural + nature combination hikes.

**Drenthe**: Open moorland, ancient forests, and minimal crowds. The Drenthe plateau offers a more remote experience than most Dutch hiking.

**Texel**: Island hiking with salt marshes, dunes, and seabird sanctuaries. Unique coastal experience within the Netherlands.

## Why Dutch Hiking?

- **Perfect trail infrastructure**: Every path is clearly marked. GPS is optional.
- **Accessibility**: Most regions are 1-2 hours from major cities.
- **Variety**: You get forests, heathlands, dunes, rivers, and valleys without traveling far.
- **Safety**: The Benelux is extraordinarily safe for hiking.
- **Weather**: Unpredictable but never extreme. Rain is common but extreme conditions are rare.

Start with Veluwe or Heuvelrug for an introduction. Progress to Zuid-Limburg for challenge. Each region teaches you something about Dutch landscapes.
