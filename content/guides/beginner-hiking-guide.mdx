Starting to hike is simple. Overdoing it isn't. This guide covers everything you need to begin safely and enjoyably.

## Choosing Your First Hike

Start small. A 5km loop in Veluwe or Heuvelrug is perfect—achievable in 1-2 hours, not psychologically overwhelming, and genuinely enjoyable.

Check the difficulty rating. "Easy" means well-marked, minimal elevation, good surfaces. "Moderate" means hills, potentially rough terrain, and 3+ hours. Save moderate for when you've completed 10+ easy hikes.

Check weather forecasts. Don't hike in heavy rain as a beginner. Choose weekdays to avoid crowds on popular routes.

## Essential Gear

- **Good boots**: Proper hiking boots prevent blisters and injuries. They're your single most important purchase. Budget €150+ for boots that'll last years.
- **Daypack**: A 22L pack is perfect. Comfortable, sized right for day hikes.
- **Hydration**: Bring 1.5-2 liters of water minimum. Dehydration causes headaches, cramping, and poor decision-making.
- **Layers**: Bring a warm layer even on warm days. Weather changes fast. A rain jacket is non-negotiable in the Benelux.
- **Food**: Snacks matter. Energy bars, fruit, nuts—anything with calories. Eat every 1-2 hours to maintain energy.
- **First aid**: Basic kit with blister prevention tape (Leukoplast), pain relief, and bandages.
- **Navigation**: Download offline maps on your phone. Don't rely on cellular service.
- **Headlamp/torch**: Even for day hikes. If you're slow, you might hike into dusk.

## Physical Preparation

Most people are fitter than they think. Walking 5-8km over easy terrain is achievable for anyone. Walking 20km over hilly terrain requires building fitness.

Start with short, easy hikes and gradually increase distance and difficulty. Your body adapts quickly—after 5-6 hikes, your baseline fitness improves dramatically.

If you have health concerns (knee issues, cardiovascular history), consult a doctor. Hiking is excellent exercise but should be approached safely.

## Safety Rules

1. **Never hike alone on unfamiliar terrain**. Accidents happen. Having a buddy provides safety and enjoyment.
2. **Tell someone where you're hiking and when to expect you back**.
3. **Turn back if conditions worsen**. Weather can deteriorate rapidly. No summit or viewpoint is worth risking.
4. **Stay on marked trails**. The Benelux has excellent trail marking. If you're unsure about directions, you're off-trail. Go back.
5. **Bring more water than you think necessary**. Dehydration impairs judgment.
6. **Don't push yourself to failure**. Hiking should be enjoyable. Suffering isn't a badge of honor.

## After Your First Hike

Your feet might be sore. This improves. Blisters will develop if you got hot spots and ignored them. Next time, tape hot spots with Leukoplast within the first hour.

Your quads might be sore from descents. This also improves. This is normal adaptation soreness, not injury.

You'll likely get hooked. Hiking provides genuine peace, achievement, and connection to nature. Once you experience that, most people become regular hikers.

The Benelux offers exceptional hiking accessibility. Take advantage of it.
