Belgium is Europe's most underrated hiking destination. The Ardennes is rugged and dramatic. The High Fens is genuinely wild. Nowhere else in the Benelux offers such variety of landscape and character.

## Top Destinations

**Ardennes**: Belgium's most dramatic landscape with deep river valleys, dense forests, and castle ruins. Serious hiking territory. Best routes: Ourthe Valley, Semois Valley.

**High Fens (Hautes Fagnes)**: Unique bogland ecosystem above 600m. Wooden boardwalks, rare plants, dramatic weather. Signal de Botrange is Belgium's highest point.

**Semois Valley**: Hidden gem with dramatic river meanders, tobacco-farming villages, and the famous GR 16 long-distance trail. Autumn colors are unmatched.

**Voerstreek**: Limestone hills on the German-Belgian border. Less touristy than Ardennes but equally beautiful.

**Flemish Ardennes**: Gentler hills and agricultural landscape in Flanders. Less dramatic than the southern Ardennes but culturally rich.

## Why Belgian Hiking?

- **Dramatic scenery**: Belgian landscapes punch above their elevation. Deep valleys create drama.
- **Authentic experience**: Less touristic than Swiss Alps or French Pyrenees but equally beautiful.
- **Cultural richness**: Medieval castles, historic towns, and traditional villages integrate with nature.
- **Hiking culture**: Belgians take hiking seriously. Trail infrastructure and signage is excellent.
- **Weather**: Unpredictable but part of the character. Mist-filled valleys are atmospheric.

Start with Hautes Fagnes for wildness. Progress to the Ardennes for challenge and drama.
