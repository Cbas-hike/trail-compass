## Overview

The Ourthe Valley is the Ardennes' hidden masterpiece—a deep, dramatic river gorge carved by limestone-sculpting waters. This 16km route follows the river through constantly shifting landscapes, with rocky outcrops, beech forests, and the medieval town of La Roche-en-Ardenne at the midpoint. The valley feels wild and remote despite being accessible by public transport, making it ideal for hikers seeking genuine Ardennes character without overambitious difficulty.

## What to expect

Starting from La Roche-en-Ardenne town center (most hikers use the town as their base), you'll initially walk through the charming main streets before heading into the valley proper. The early section ascends through mixed forest to reach viewpoints above the town, offering your first dramatic perspective of the valley and the Ourthe river winding below.

The route then descends and follows the river closely for roughly 8km. This is the route's highlight section. The river itself is constant—sometimes barely 10 meters wide, flowing over limestone bedrock, clear water visible. The valley walls rise steeply on both sides, creating a 100-200 meter-high canyon. Beech forest dominates, with occasional rocky outcrops breaking through vegetation.

Several dramatic "rochers" (rocky viewpoints) punctuate the riverside walk, offering perspective from above the river's sinuous meanders. Each viewpoint reveals another tight loop in the river below—the Ourthe's path is anything but direct. The limestone bedrock creates pools in the river where locals swim in summer.

Wildlife encounters are common in the early morning: roe deer frequent the riverside, kingfishers hunt the pools, and occasionally you'll spot evidence of wild boar. The forest is quiet and enveloping.

The terrain itself is occasionally technical. The path includes steep scrambles, rooted sections, and rocky steps. Nothing climbing-grade, but requires good balance and careful footing, particularly on wet days. The river can be crossed on a suspension footbridge at the route's midpoint, adding a bit of adventure.

The return route follows the opposite valley wall, ascending through forest and passing back above La Roche before descending into town. This section is less dramatic than the riverside walk but offers good vantage points back across the valley.

## Route highlights

- **Ourthe river canyon**: The river's tight meanders create constantly changing perspectives—each bend reveals new dramatic rock formations
- **La Roche-en-Ardenne medieval town**: Castle ruins, cobblestone streets, riverside restaurants and cafes at the 8km midpoint
- **Rocky outcrops (rochers)**: Limestone cliffs and viewpoints scattered throughout offer dramatic valley perspectives

## Practical information

**Starting point:** La Roche-en-Ardenne town center. Multiple parking options (€2/hour or €12/day all-day). GPS: 50.2082°N, 5.2259°E

**Parking:** Town center has metered parking and large paid lots. Often busy on weekends. Alternative: park outside town center and walk 20 minutes in

**Public transport:** Train from Brussels or Liège to La Roche-en-Ardenne (via Libramont), bus connections available. Return buses frequent

**Facilities:** La Roche has excellent infrastructure—restaurants, cafes, shops, tourist office. Multiple places to eat during the midpoint town section. Toilets available

**Route variant:** This can be extended to 25km by including the Semois valley adjacent trail system, available through the local hiking network

## Best time to go

**Spring (April-May):** Fresh growth, wildflowers in meadows, river levels high from snowmelt, temperatures moderate (12-16°C). Can be muddy

**Summer (June-August):** Warm (18-24°C) and dry, perfect conditions. River swimmable for cooling off. Very crowded, particularly July

**Autumn (September-October):** Ideal season. Beech forest in full golden/russet colors, temperatures perfect (10-16°C), morning mist fills valley creating mystical atmosphere, crowds moderate

**Winter (November-March):** Cold, damp, occasional icy sections. Not recommended except for winter enthusiasts

## Difficulty & fitness

This is a moderate route requiring solid fitness and good balance. The 16km distance is substantial but manageable with two 8km sections separated by the La Roche-en-Ardenne lunch stop. The terrain is sometimes technical—there are steep scrambles, rooted sections, and rocky steps, particularly on the riverbank sections. Nothing grade-II scrambling, but better suited to experienced hikers than complete beginners.

Expected fitness level: Comfortable with 4-5 hour days, experienced with rocky/technical terrain, able to manage steep descent sections safely.

## Tips & warnings

- **Good boots essential:** The rooted, rocky terrain demands solid ankle support. Trail runners insufficient
- **Wet rock is slippery:** After rain, the limestone becomes extremely slippery. Avoid for 2 days post-rain
- **River crossing can be tricky:** The suspension footbridge is thrilling but not difficult. Very high water in spring can make it bounce slightly
- **Afternoon crowds:** La Roche fills with tourists in afternoons. Eat lunch early (before 1pm) for better restaurant experience
- **Bring water:** There's water access in La Roche but nowhere else. Carry 1.5+ liters
- **Headlamp useful:** Early autumn mornings have thick mist filling the valley—a headlamp helps with poor visibility
- **Swimming spots:** Summer hikers often use river pools for cooling off. Currents can be strong; exercise caution
- **Solitude timing:** Start very early (7am) to avoid afternoon crowds and share the valley with wildlife
