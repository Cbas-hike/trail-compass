## Overview

The Posbank is the Veluwe's crown jewel—a long heathery ridge rising 100 meters above the surrounding landscape with panoramic views across the IJssel valley. This 12km loop offers the perfect half-day hike, combining open ridgetop walking with dramatic sand dunes and quiet forest trails. It's ideal for family groups and those seeking stunning views without excessive technical difficulty.

## What to expect

Starting from the Posbank parking area near Rheden, you'll begin with a gentle climb through mixed oak and pine forest. The initial section is shaded and pleasant, gaining elevation steadily as you approach the ridge proper. After about 3km, you emerge onto the open heathland that characterizes the Posbank summit—your first views here are exhilarating.

The ridge walk itself is the route's highlight. You'll traverse roughly 3km of open heathland with unobstructed 360-degree views. To the west lies the patchwork of Veluwe farmland and forest; to the east, the IJssel river valley spreads below you, with the Arnhem hills visible beyond. In late August and September, the entire ridge turns purple with flowering heather—an unforgettable sight.

The descent leads through the Beekhuizerzand (literally "Beekhuizen sand"), a shifting dune landscape that feels entirely alien to the Netherlands. These white sand dunes, some up to 20 meters high, were created during the last ice age and continue to shift slowly. Sparse grasses, low shrubs, and hardy pines cling to the slopes. The path winds through this dramatic terrain before re-entering forest for the final return to the parking area.

Wildlife is abundant throughout. Red squirrels are commonly spotted, particularly in the forest sections. Fallow deer frequent the heathland edges, and wild boar tracks are evident everywhere. Early morning starts increase your chances of wildlife encounters.

## Route highlights

- **Posbank ridge panorama**: The 3km ridgetop walk offers some of the Netherlands' most dramatic valley views, with the IJssel river snaking below
- **Beekhuizerzand sand dunes**: Otherworldly white dunes that feel more Saharan than Dutch, creating a complete landscape change
- **Heathland in bloom**: August and September transform the ridge into a continuous purple carpet of flowering heather

## Practical information

**Starting point:** Posbank parking area (paid parking, €5/day), Rheden. GPS: 51.9960°N, 5.8542°E

**Parking:** Dedicated parking area with about 150 spaces. Very busy on weekends and weekday afternoons—arrive early. Alternative parking available in Rheden town center (20 minute walk to trailhead)

**Public transport:** Direct bus from Arnhem city center (bus 3, 40 minutes) to Posbank. Return bus available, check RTM schedules

**Facilities:** Café at parking area (open weekends year-round, weekdays in summer). No facilities on the route itself—bring all water and snacks. Toilets at parking area

## Best time to go

**Spring (April-May):** Fresh green growth, wildflowers in meadows, fewer crowds. Temperatures 12-16°C. Some muddy sections after rain.

**Summer (June-August):** Warm and dry, perfect for the open ridge. July-August very busy. Temperatures 16-22°C. Bring sun protection and water.

**Autumn (September-October):** Peak heathland bloom (purple flowers) through mid-September. Excellent colors, moderate crowds. Temperatures 10-16°C.

**Winter:** Rarely snow, but muddy and gray. Not recommended except for winter naturalists.

## Difficulty & fitness

This is an easy route suitable for families with children (age 8+) and recreational hikers. The elevation gain is only 180m and distributed over the first half of the route. The ridge walk is on well-maintained paths with no scrambling or technical sections. A few steep sandy descents in the Beekhuizerzand require care but aren't difficult.

Expected fitness: Moderate pace suitable for mixed-ability groups. No rock scrambling or route-finding challenges.

## Tips & warnings

- **Bring lots of water:** The open ridge provides zero shade. On summer days, bring 2-3 liters per person
- **Start early:** Weekends see the parking area full by 10am. Starting by 8am ensures good parking and solitude on the ridge
- **Muddy in winter:** After rain, forest sections become boggy. Waterproof boots recommended November-March
- **Wildlife is wild:** While squirrels and deer are harmless, wild boar can be aggressive if surprised with piglets. Hike in groups and make noise
- **No resupply:** There's no water or food on the route. Pack a full lunch and snacks
- **Sun exposure:** The ridge offers no shelter. Sun protection (hat, sunscreen, sunglasses) essential even on cool days
