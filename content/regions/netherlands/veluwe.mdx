The Veluwe is where North European nature feels truly wild. Massive stretches of heathland (1,000 km² total), rare species of moss and plant life, sand drifts that belong in the Sahara, and dense forests creating genuine remoteness despite being just 90 minutes from Amsterdam. This region is unique in Western Europe—a functioning ecosystem rather than a manicured park.

The Hoge Veluwe National Park is the crown jewel—the Netherlands' only national park with an entrance fee (worth every euro). The Kröller-Müller Museum houses one of Europe's finest modern art collections, including the world's second-largest Van Gogh collection, displayed in forest clearings. The Museonder underground exhibition explores the region's unique geology and biology. Free bicycles (provided with park entry) let you cover more distance through the 50+ kilometers of maintained trails. The park's white-tailed deer population sometimes exceeds 2,000 individuals—seeing herds graze in meadows is unforgettable.

The heathland landscape is most dramatic in August-September when the heather blooms in a continuous purple carpet across the landscape. A million purple flowers transform the landscape into something more reminiscent of Scandinavia than the Netherlands. Spring (April-May) brings wildflower displays and migrating birds. October offers golden autumn colors and the most comfortable hiking temperatures.

The Posbank is the region's most famous viewpoint—a long heathland ridge offering panoramic views across the IJssel river valley to the Arnhem hills. The Beekhuizerzand (literally "Beekhuizen sand") features shifting white sand dunes up to 20 meters high, creating a landscape that feels like the Sahara rather than the Low Countries. These dunes continue to shift gradually due to wind patterns, and the sparse vegetation (grasses and low shrubs) struggles to stabilize them—a rare ecological phenomenon so close to major cities.

The hiking infrastructure here is impeccable. Well-maintained trail networks, excellent public transport (trains and buses serve all major towns), abundant accommodations from budget to luxury, and numerous cafes and restaurants throughout the region. Family-friendly trails suit younger hikers; longer routes challenge experienced walkers. The region is perfect for weekend day hikes from Amsterdam, Utrecht, or Arnhem, or as a multi-day hiking base.
